Research Statement

My research trajectory has evolved from investigating micro-scale spatial perception to systematic modeling of complex urban systems. Currently, my work focuses on the coupling mechanisms between transit-oriented development (TOD) and multi-dimensional spatial performance within high-density metro hubs. By constructing a comprehensive framework analyzing efficiency, spatial vitality, and urban resilience, I employ digital twin technologies, computational simulation, and multi-objective optimization methodologies to drive the intelligent renovation of underground and station-city environments. I am dedicated to translating high-fidelity empirical data and systemic insights into actionable architectural and urban design strategies.

Research Focus

01

Station-City Integration & Spatial Performance

Studying the systematic interconnections between transit infrastructure, underground architectural spaces, and urban self-organization through the theoretical lens of multi-dimensional performance optimization.

02

Digital Twin & Intelligent Renovation

Investigating the intelligent retrofitting methodologies for high-density metro hub environments, utilizing digital twin coupling, space syntax, and computational simulation to optimize underground space performance.

03

Spatial Perception & Human-Centered Optimization

Analyzing micro- and meso-scale user behaviors, cognitive mapping, and spatial perception under high-density constraints to inform performance-driven, human-centered urban design.

04

Empirical Evaluation & Practical Design

Developing data-driven diagnostic toolkits and evaluation frameworks that translate complex theoretical models and empirical field data into practical, resilient spatial design solutions.
